Rabbit Upper Airway Disease

Reference guide with built-in treatment calculator.

1. Typical Clinical Findings

Rabbits often hide disease. Visible upper airway signs usually indicate an established process.

  • Breathing pattern: Increased respiratory rate/effort. Open-mouth breathing is an emergency sign.
  • Nasal discharge: Serous to mucopurulent/caseous. Check forepaws for sticky, dirty fur.
  • Auscultation: Upper airway noise may transmit into thorax and mimic primary lung sounds.
  • Dental exam: Evaluate roots and asymmetry; root disease may extend into nasal cavity/tear duct.
  • Ophthalmology: Epiphora/dacryocystitis can be secondary to root disease or intranasal inflammation.
2. Diagnostics

If recurrent disease or poor response, investigate for anatomical differentials.

  • CT skull/thorax: Gold standard for root pathology, nasal destruction, bullae changes, masses and deep abscesses.
  • Radiography (4-5 views): Moderate sensitivity, useful for gross root and thoracic changes.
  • Superficial nasal swab: Low clinical value due to contamination.
  • Deep nasal flush under sedation: Split sample to culture + PCR; PCR helps during ongoing antibiotics.
