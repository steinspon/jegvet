# Euthanasia of reptiles

Humane destruction of reptiles can be difficult. They have a high tolerance of hypoxia and it is possible for a reptile euthanased with pentobarbitone to survive for some time. Because of this all reptiles that have been euthanased or are believed to be dead (end stage disease) should have their CNS destroyed after euthanasia (just in case).

Pentobarbitione can be given, ideally, via the intravenous route. This can be intracardiac in collapsed lizards or snakes. If the reptile is lively then premedication with a high dose of ketamine is possible to facilitate euthanasia. In chelonians access is possible via the epicoelomic area with a long needle. I would check for cardiovascular collapse using the Doppler and once the heart has stopped pith the animal via the roof of the mouth, although there is actually no need to wait for the heart to stop. Skeletal muscle undergoes rigor mortis before cardiac muscle so a reptile can be presented in rigor with a heart beat but it has been cortically dead for some time. The only occasion where pithing should be avoided is when the CNS is required for histopathology. In this case I would perform a post mortem promptly, remove the CNS and fix it.
