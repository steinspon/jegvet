# Ultrasound CT and MRI

Ultrasound is useful in reptiles. Probes with a small footprint are required. 7.5MHZ scanners are best for most situations. Snakes can be scanned along their entire length from the ventral aspect. Lizards can be scanned from the ventral aspect or from the thoracic inlet. Chelonians can be scanned from the prefemoral fossae or between the head and legs. They are most useful to assess the reproductive status of female chelonians.

CT and MRI also have their place but are limited in practice. Chelonian fractures are easily visualised using CT as there is no superimposition of multiple shell sections.
