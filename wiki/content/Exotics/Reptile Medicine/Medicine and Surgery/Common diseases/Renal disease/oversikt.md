# Renal disease

Renal disease is common in captive reptiles. They are typically uricotelic and produce uric acid. They have a renal portal system which assures tubular blood flow in periods of dehydration when glomerular filtration is reduced. However uric acid is actively excreted and this can block tubules leading to damage. Chronic disease is typical with fibrosis and tubulonephrosis common. In these cases the levels of uric acid in the plasma can increase with subsequent visceral or articular gout. Clinical signs are non specific and many cases are detected on blood results when already severe when severe end stage renal disease is present. Reversed calcium to phosphorous ratios or elevations in uric acid are typical findings. If mild treatment with allopurinol, a xanthine oxidase inhibitor, can reduce the production of uric acid allowing proteins to be excreted as xanthine and hypoxanthine. Many cases are left on allopurinol long term. Fluid therapy and phosphate binders may also be indicated. Endoscopic biopsy is indicated for a histological diagnosis.
