# Upper respiratory tract disease in chelonians

Runny nose syndrome (RNS) and stomatitis (mouth rot) are historic diseases of tortoises. These were attributed to poor husbandry. Many pathogens can be involved and will occur in both good and bad husbandry. Clinical signs include conjunctivitis, rhinitis, stomatitis, dyspnoea, respiratory stridor and noise. Caseous discharges can accumulate. Secondary infections with bacterial and fungal agents are common.
