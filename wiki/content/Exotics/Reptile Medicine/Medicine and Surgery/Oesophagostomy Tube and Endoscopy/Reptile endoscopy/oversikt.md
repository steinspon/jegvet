# Reptile endoscopy

Endoscopic examination is an under utilised tool in reptile medicine. There are a variety of possible routes to consider. Endoscopic examination of the oesophagus, trachea and cloaca are possible. Examination of the coelomic cavity is possible in all species. In squamates exploratory surgery provides a more thorough assessment of the coelomic cavity.

In chelonians the shell prevents access to the body cavity without major trauma. Endoscopic examination via the prefemoral fossa allows an assessment of the coelomic cavity using a soft tissue approach. The lung fields, kidneys and muscle need to be avoided. Thus an entry point mid fossa is ideal. The skin is incised and a blunt instrument can be used to puncture into the coelomic cavity. Having the tortoise on it’s side should encourage the bladder to fall away from the entry point limiting any complications. With careful placement of the incision it is possible to gain entry into the lung fields as well. If this is performed then the horizontal membrane should be closed to prevent pneumocoelom developing. A single horizontal mattress suture can be used to close the surgical wound.

In lizards the exact entry point depends on what you wish to assess. The standard approach is from the left side in the sub lumbar area, below the spine, in front of the hind leg and behind the rib cage. Care has to be taken in those species where the lungs can extend this far back (chameleons). The skin is incised and a blunt instrument can be used to puncture into the coelomic cavity. Tenting the skin up minimises the risk of visceral puncture. Carbon dioxide insufflation allows a greater evaluation of the lizard. A single horizontal mattress suture can be used to close the surgical wound. The ventral midline approach can be used for a more complete assessment of the liver.

Snake endoscopic examination to visualise the lung fields and organ biopsy via the air sac is possible. The entry site is on the right side between the ribs. Insufflation is not required.

Apart from visualisation, biopsies can be used for culture, histopathology, cytology or PCR examination.
