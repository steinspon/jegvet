Force-feeding planning at total dose 80 ml/kg/day.

1. Clinical Indication and Goal

Start Critical Care force-feeding promptly with anorexia/reduced appetite secondary to upper airway obstruction, pain or early GI stasis.

Goal: preserve continuous GI motility. Typical duration is 1-3 days, or until normal voluntary feeding returns.

Do not start force-feeding with confirmed mechanical complete gastric/intestinal obstruction (physical ileus), especially if blood glucose is > 15 mmol/L.

2. Preparation and Storage

  • Mix 1 part Critical Care powder with 2-3 parts lukewarm water to a smooth slurry.
  • Avoid boiling water, since high heat reduces vitamin and probiotic quality.
  • Prepared slurry: keep refrigerated in closed container, max 24 hours. Stir before each feeding. Discard leftovers after 24 hours.
  • Opened powder: up to 30 days at room temperature (well sealed), or up to 6 months in freezer.

3. Important Feeding Technique Warning

Be very gentle and slow when inserting/removing the syringe. With a 1 ml syringe, a rabbit may need up to 120 insertions/day.

Use diastema gently. Give small portions (up to 0.5 ml) and confirm swallowing before each next portion.
