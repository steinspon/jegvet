Dog B2 Cardiac Sedation

Based on the mitral insufficiency anaesthesia protocol. Do not use in dogs with congestive heart failure.

Special considerations

  • This protocol should not be used in dogs with congestive heart failure. Consult an anaesthesiologist in such cases.
  • If medical treatment is indicated, it should be started before anaesthesia. Continue ongoing cardiac medication, but avoid ACE inhibitors on the day of anaesthesia because of increased risk of hypotension.
  • Comorbidity must be considered when choosing drugs. Acepromazine should be avoided in bleeding, SIRS/sepsis or severe BOAS.

Calculation information

Doses from the protocol used in this calculator:

  • Acepromazine: 0.01-0.02 mg/kg IM
  • Methadone for painful procedures: 0.2-0.4 mg/kg IM
  • Butorphanol for non-painful procedures: 0.2-0.4 mg/kg IM
  • Propofol for induction: 3-5 mg/kg IV
  • Propofol for TIVA: 12-36 mg/kg/hour
  • Fentanyl: 5-20 µg/kg/hour
  • Ketamine bolus: 0.5 mg/kg IV as needed
  • Ketamine CRI: 0.6 mg/kg/hour

The following concentrations are used for volume calculations: acepromazine 10 mg/ml, methadone 10 mg/ml, butorphanol 10 mg/ml, propofol 10 mg/ml, fentanyl 0.05 mg/ml (50 µg/ml), and ketamine 10 mg/ml.

Formula: Volume (ml) = dose (mg/kg) × weight (kg) / concentration (mg/ml). Infusion rate (ml/h) = dose rate (mg/kg/h) × weight (kg) / concentration (mg/ml).
