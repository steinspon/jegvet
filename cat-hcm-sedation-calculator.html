Cat HCM Sedation

Anaesthesia protocol for cats with hypertrophic cardiomyopathy (HCM). Not for use in congestive heart failure.

Special considerations

  • Do not use this protocol for cats in congestive heart failure — consult an anaesthesiologist.
  • Continue existing cardiac medications. Avoid ACE inhibitors on the day of anaesthesia (increased hypotension risk).
  • Consider comorbidities when selecting drugs. Aim for euthyroid status before anaesthesia if hyperthyroidism is present.
  • Avoid tachycardia and vasodilation. Do not use ephedrine or drugs that increase contractility (e.g. dobutamine).

Calculation information

Drug concentrations used in this calculator:

  • Dexmedetomidine: 0.5 mg/ml
  • Methadone: 10 mg/ml
  • Butorphanol: 10 mg/ml
  • Propofol (induction): 10 mg/ml
  • Propofol (TIVA): 10 mg/ml
  • Alfaxalone: 10 mg/ml
  • Fentanyl: 0.05 mg/ml (50 µg/ml)
  • Ketamine: 10 mg/ml (diluted from standard solution for practical syringe pump volumes)

Formulas: Volume (ml) = dose (mg/kg) × weight (kg) / concentration (mg/ml). Infusion rate (ml/h) = dose rate (mg/kg/h) × weight (kg) / concentration (mg/ml).
